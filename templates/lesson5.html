LESSON 5 :Life Cycles and Traits

Exploring the interconnected changes in organisms and the environment

Life Cycles

A life cycle is the sequence of biological changes that occurs as an organism develops from an egg into an adult until its death. Many species synchronize their life cycles with environmental changes, such as the seasons, to enhance survival.

  • Example: Many butterfly life cycles align with the flowering times of plants.
  • Environmental triggers, such as temperature, impact the transitions between stages.
  • Disruptions in these transitions can affect ecosystems, such as mismatched pollination timing.

Traits

Traits are the characteristics of an organism, including its appearance and functions. Traits can be morphological, physiological, or behavioral, and are shaped by interactions with the environment.

  • Traits are inherited through genetic material (DNA).
  • They play a key role in interactions with other organisms and survival in changing environments.

Environmental Changes

Life cycles and traits are influenced by various Earth processes and environmental factors, such as:

  • Evolutionary processes affecting populations over generations.
  • Species interactions for food, shelter, and competition.
  • Climatic conditions like sunlight, temperature, and precipitation.
